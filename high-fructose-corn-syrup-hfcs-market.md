<p><p>High Fructose Corn Syrup (HFCS) is primarily available in two main types: HFCS-42 and HFCS-55. HFCS-42 contains 42% fructose and is often used in products like salad dressings and sauces. In contrast, HFCS-55, with 55% fructose, is commonly found in soft drinks and sweetened beverages. Additionally, there are other variants, such as HFCS-90, which contains 90% fructose and is used in specialty food applications. These different types cater to varying sweetness levels and food industry needs.</p></p>
